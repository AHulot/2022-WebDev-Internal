Basic Internet Safety

When using the Internet, it is important to protect your identity and to stay safe online.

Here are a few basic tips to help you do this.


1. Use Secure Devices to Protect Personal Information

Protect your devices using security options like passwords, screen locks, or fingerprint readers. This will help prevent anyone from accessing your own private information.

2. Use Strong Passwords

Make sure your password meets the requirement of a strong password. Try to include capital letters, symbols, or punctuation to make your password unique and hard to guess. Do not share these passwords with other people and make sure to change them often. More about passwords…

3. Update Apps and Software

When software and apps are updated, bugs and security issues are often fixed. By updating your apps and software, the software and apps you use will become more reliable and secure. This can help reduce exposure to viruses and malware. More about malware…

4. Use Secure Wifi Networks

Public wifi is usually unsafe to use. Through unsecured wifi, hackers might be able to send malware to your device or access personal information. It is better to avoid using any public wifi. Any wifi networks you own should be secured with a strong password that should be changed often and not shared with anyone else.

5. Back Up and Save All Personal Data

In case of an emergency, back up any important personal information on external hard drives. This will allow you to save your important information in case you happen to lose all the data on your device.
